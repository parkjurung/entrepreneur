### 2026-06-15 - Alcanthia 3-Month Validation Gate

- Context: Alcanthia currently has DAU around 200, ROAS around 150%, daily ad spend around KRW 160K, daily average payment revenue around KRW 220K, and D7 retention around 10-15%. The game is above BEP but not yet profitable enough after platform fees and VAT to justify shifting heavy company weight onto it. The company has current cash flow from AI tutor outsourced work and Pagecall legacy SaaS, so Alcanthia is a growth-option decision rather than an immediate survival decision.
- Options considered:
  - Treat Alcanthia as the company's primary bet immediately.
  - Keep Alcanthia as a side project only.
  - Run a time-boxed validation push with explicit retention and profitability gates.
  - Stop Alcanthia and move quickly to a new game.
- Decision: Run a roughly 3-month validation push. Target stable D7 retention around 18-22% in recent paid-acquisition cohorts while preserving ROAS, payer conversion, ARPPU, and longer-term retention quality. Do not treat a one-off D7 20% spike as sufficient validation.
- Rationale: D7 20% is a useful first gate for whether Alcanthia's early loop is strong enough to deserve more company weight. Current D7 10-15% indicates real signal but not yet enough confidence. The leading product hypothesis is that users reach the automation / offline-progress aha moment too late; this is testable within 3 months. A time-box avoids both premature abandonment and open-ended investment.
- Next actions: Clarify the funnel to first farm, first brew, first adventure, first automation/offline reward, and first payment; run 2-3 product experiments to move automation aha earlier; validate the best variant with paid cohorts; review D1/D7/D14/D30, ROAS, payer conversion, and ARPPU together.
- Review date: 2026-09-15

### 2026-06-15 - Open US Immigration And Ecosystem Access Track

- Context: The user is concerned that frontier AI, deeptech, energy, and national-security-adjacent technology may increasingly be controlled by US-led ecosystems. The user's current identity is a Korea-born Korean founder operating a Korea-based company, while long-term ambitions include large-company building and civilizational-scale domains such as AI, energy, quantum technology, and the future of humanity.
- Options considered:
  - Ignore US immigration and continue as a Korea-only founder.
  - Immediately prioritize emigration over current company execution.
  - Treat US immigration and ecosystem access as a deliberate parallel option while preserving current execution and compounding assets.
- Decision: Open a US immigration and ecosystem access track as a 12-36 month strategic project. Do not treat it as an immediate escape plan or as a reason to abandon the current company.
- Rationale: US access may become a major career and strategic option value for a frontier-oriented founder. However, the user's strongest near-term leverage is still current company execution, cash flow, AI tutor / realtime agent proof, and eventual exit or credibility accumulation. The right move is to convert those assets into US-legible evidence, relationships, and immigration options.
- Next actions: Consult a US startup / AI immigration lawyer; evaluate O-1A, EB-1A, EB-2 NIW, E-2, and company/customer/investor foothold paths; create an evidence checklist; prepare English-facing Pagecall / AI tutor technical and business narrative; start US network conversations with founders, researchers, customers, investors, and immigration professionals.
