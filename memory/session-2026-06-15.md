## 2. Strategic Baseline

The company is intentionally in an "anomie" state. It is avoiding a high-inertia identity while looking for the next durable opportunity.

Current posture:

- Keep Pagecall B2B SaaS as legacy revenue while it lasts.

- Treat Alcanthia as one candidate for that larger opportunity.

The user believes the agent era weakens the defensibility of selling software itself. Customers can internalize more software development, and token-rich category leaders may compound faster. By contrast, games may remain viable without being number one because tastes are diverse and persona-driven.

## 7. Alcanthia Product Thesis

Alcanthia is a pixel-art simulation RPG centered on farming, brewing/alchemy, adventure, crafting, automation, and player trading.

Core loop:

1. Plant and cultivate crops.
2. Harvest materials.
3. Brew or synthesize.
4. Send adventurers.
5. Use loot to craft/enhance.
6. Expand productivity and automation.
7. Trade with other players to pursue goals through different economic specializations.

Target users:

- Men and women in their 20s to 40s.
- Likes pixel art, RPGs, and automation.

Retention reason:

- Users return to see results of automated farming.
- Users pursue self-defined goals such as high-enhancement tools or expanding field productivity.

Current leading D7 retention hypothesis:

- The automation aha moment comes too late.
- Players must endure too much before they understand why the game becomes compelling.
- The likely product question is not just "add more content" but "move the automation taste earlier without collapsing long-term progression."
